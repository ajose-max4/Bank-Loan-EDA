# Bank Loan Performance Report  
## Good vs Bad Loans Analysis

### Overview
This report summarizes bank loan performance by distinguishing between good loans and bad loans. Good loans are defined as loans that are fully paid or currently active, while bad loans refer to charged-off loans that were not successfully repaid.

The dashboard provides insights into loan performance across states, loan grades (A–G risk levels), and loan purposes.

---

| Metric | Value |
|------|------|
| Total Loan Applications | 38.6K |
| Total Funded Amount | $435.8M |

The total amount received exceeds the funded amount, indicating that interest payments contribute significantly to overall revenue generated from loans.

---

# Risk Insights

### Interest Rate and Risk
Higher-risk loans generally carry higher interest rates. This is reflected in the fact that charged-off loans have higher interest rates than fully paid loans, and current loans show the highest average interest rates.

This suggests lenders price loans based on borrower risk level.

### Debt-to-Income Ratio
Bad loans tend to have slightly higher average debt-to-income ratios, indicating that borrowers with more debt relative to income may have higher default risk.

---

# Filtering Dimensions

The dashboard allows further analysis using three filters.

### State
Loan performance can be examined across different states to identify regional patterns in loan risk and repayment behavior.

### Grade (A–G)
Loan grades represent borrower risk levels.

| Grade | Risk Level |
|---|---|
| A | Lowest Risk |
| B–C | Moderate Risk |
| D–E | Higher Risk |
| F–G | Highest Risk |

Higher letter grades generally correspond with higher interest rates and increased default risk.

### Purpose
Loans are categorized by purpose, such as debt consolidation, home improvement, small business, and personal expenses.

Analyzing loan performance by loan purpose helps identify which types of borrowing carry the most risk.

---

# Key Business Insights

1. Loan portfolio health is strong, with over 86 percent of loans performing well.
2. Bad loans still represent nearly $66M in funded capital, highlighting the importance of risk management.
3. Higher interest rates correlate with higher default risk, suggesting lenders price risk appropriately.
4. Borrower financial health, measured through debt-to-income ratio, influences loan outcomes.

---

# Conclusion

The loan portfolio shows strong overall performance, with the majority of loans being successfully repaid or remaining active. However, a portion of loans becomes charged off, representing potential financial losses.

Monitoring risk factors such as loan grade, borrower debt levels, and loan purpose can help lenders improve lending strategies and reduce default rates.
